Project Overview

Motivation and Design Problem

Making friends with similar struggles, interests, and cultural backgrounds in college is a shared common difficulty that most people have. Many will join cultural clubs with the purpose of mingling, getting to know more people, and hoping to learn more about their cultures. However, most of the time, these cultural clubs are just too big of a group to actually have one-on-one conversations with others. There are also groups on Facebook, such as Subtle Asian Traits and its variations, however these groups mainly consist of meme-tagging with your friends and it doesn’t really allow for learning about culture and making friends. Additionally, since the dominant culture in America is the western culture, it is easy for one, such as an Asian descent, to lose touch with their cultural roots. With these problems in mind, I wanted to make a product that intends to connect people together with similar backgrounds and interests while promoting cultural connectedness and awareness.


Methods & Process

I will do a heuristic evaluation of current apps to draw comparisons to what can be improved and what is good about the app. For the contextual inquiry, I will start interviewing the target users to gather data about their current methods on connecting and meeting new people and what they look for in meeting new people. After obtaining data, I will use UX Storytelling methods to ideate a solution. I will then create a flowchart, wireframe/lo-fi prototype for visualizations and then testing out the wireframe with random people to look for improvements. With the feedback, I hope to make revisions to improve the project.


Expected Outcome

I will present my work by documentation on a website with my process of this research project, and using my high-fidelity prototype as an example of the research work.

Literature Review

Friendships consist of a mutual and affectionate relationship between two friends. Under this term “friendship”, there are many different types, and more recently emerging are online friendships. Online friendships are developed in online social environments, such as chat rooms or social media, through computer-mediated communications (CMC). In these online environments, such as Facebook, it is suggested that there are possible mental health and well-being benefits that are associated with the feeling of connectedness to others (Grieve et al., 2013). Although online friendships tend to develop slower initially, after passing a critical period, which is around six months to a year, the quality of the online friendship grows quickly (Chan and Cheng, 2000). This feeling of connectedness, belongingness, and relatedness is called social connectedness (Smolders & De Kort, 2009). Social connectedness contributes to greater subjective well-being because it acts as a mediator for how people come to understand their social experiences, which influences their relationship-enhancing behaviors (Lee et al., 2008).

In America, Asians may feel, think, and act American, however, they are repeatedly treated as if they are aliens and do not belong in America to the same degree as other Americans (Cheryan & Monin, 2005). Asian Americans are suggested to be more likely to be mistaken for being from another country or be a non-native English speaker compared to White Americans (Cheryan & Monin, 2005). In other words, the repeated exclusion from society that Asian Americans go through can greatly impact their psychology and behavior. This study explores the factors of empowering cultural connectedness through online friendships. Cultural connectedness can mean different things in different contexts. However, specifically for this study, cultural connectedness encompasses how much Asian descents are and feel integrated with their heritage and ethnic culture. The more a person feels culturally connected to their respected culture may suggest a higher self-efficacy, sense of self regarding time, school connectedness, and life satisfaction (Snowshoe 2015). Therefore, this study will investigate the interconnection between an individual's cultural heritage and cultural community with respect to cultural connectedness. The study findings will contribute to the cultural studies of online behavior especially how to model to establish online friends with similar struggles, interests, and cultural backgrounds.

What are the things users would do with the app?

The main activity my project will support for the users is connecting Asian descents through an app in hopes to promote cultural awareness. According to Maslow's hierarchy of needs, the basic needs involved for this project are psychological, which includes the feeling of belongingness and love, such as friendships, romantic relationships, trust and acceptance, family, and a sense of connectedness. The traditional solutions to satisfy people's need for belongingness and love ranges from big to small groups. Some examples of big groups include clubs, sports events, and work. Additionally, some examples of smaller groups that satisfy these needs are forming friendships, romantic relationships and family.

Some possible limitations with the traditional methods for satisfying the psychological needs for belongingness and love are that people are limited with people in their area. For my project, I am aiming to create a platform for Asians descents to get connected to promote cultural awareness and connectedness, since it is common for an individual's community not to have a cultural center or a large demographics of people that share the same cultural background. On another note, more and more apps and social media are enabling people to connect over the internet. In other words, people can connect virtually in the form of online friendships and romantic relationships. So, although the idea of connecting online is not something completely new, I hope to utilize this technology to help with the limitation in which people do not have to be limited to their area in order to connect with people who share the same cultural background as them.


Target Users

The nature of this study is to look into Asian descents, so regarding the inclusion criteria for my target users, this study will be limited to Asians. The age criteria will be adults who are competent to give informed consent, so 18 years or older. Also, gender is non-discriminatory. In the consent form, the participants are screened to see if they understand the written consent information and assume that the participant who checks and agrees will have at least intermediate proficiency in English. In a typical setting, the user will use this app during their free time, typically in the morning or during the evening. The user will open the app to socialize and to learn more about their culture.


Research Design and Methods

A factor analysis was used for the initial analysis. Therefore, by the rule of thumb by calculating 10 subjects per variable (10x20 questions), 200 is an optimized-ideal target sample size. 200 participants were invited via an online forum and were asked to answer 20 questions regarding the attitude toward cultural heritage and online socialization. These questions are on a likert-scale: “Strongly Disagree”, “Disagree”, “Neutral”, “Agree” and “Strongly Agree”. However by assuming the 20% of total participants may drop or be removed during the cleanup process due to invalid/missing data rate, I recruited 250 participants. Personal reflection about the participant's experience in the cultural community will be asked by six open-ended questions. The survey would take approximately 15 minutes to complete and each participant had only one chance to complete the survey.

The findings of this research will contribute to the appreciation of diversity in society by encouraging cultural connectedness and by promoting cultural awareness and identity.The results of the research may inspire more research regarding cultural connectedness and ways to promote cultural awareness and identity.


Results

Using Microsoft Excel, I graphed all the responses from the survey after quantifying the likert-scale. This figure shows the mean, median and standard deviation for each question (20 questions total).

Using R, I plotted a line plot of the eigenvalues of factors or principal components in an analysis. There are 20 components because of the 20 questions.

Using R, I did a factor analysis on the 6 factors found from doing principle component analysis (PCA). For the loadings, my cut off value to determine significance was 0.3, such that if the loading was less than 0.3, I would consider them insignificant. To find the communalities, I subtracted 1 from the uniqueness. My cut off value was 0.5, such that if the communalities were less than 0.5, I would consider them insignificant.

Each factor explains a certain percentage of variance. I used a donut chart to visualize how much variance is explained by each factor.

"Online Connections" had high loadings with these items from the survey: I made online friends before; I have had good experiences before from meeting people through social media.

"Cultural Learning" had high loadings with these items: meeting more people with the same cultural background with me will make me feel more connected to my culture; being more culturally connected would make me feel more content; having a cultural community would be helpful in learning about my culture; making online friends with the same cultural background as me is a great way for me to learn more about my culture; making online friends is a great way to connect with people from other places; I spend more than half my time on the internet on social media.

"Physical Cultural Community" had high loadings with these items: have friends with the same cultural backgrounds; besides from family, I have a lot of exposure to my culture; there are a lot of people with the same cultural background as me in the area I live in.

"Language Building" had high loadings with these items: I am literate in my cultural language's writing system; I am fluent in my cultural language.

"Online Safety" had high loadings with: I feel unsafe making online friends; I distrust the internet for making online friends; I have had bad experiences before from meeting people through social media.

"Cultural Exposure" had high loadings with: I took a language course that is my cultural language./I am currently taking a language that is my cultural language; I attended a cultural school before/I am currently attending one.

The communalities of the variables included a few high variances: I am literate in my cultural language's writing system; I distrust the internet for making online friends. This may mean that the proportion of each variable's variance can only be mediocrely explained by these factors, since only about half of the variables have 0.5 or greater communalities. However, the test of the hypothesis stated that six factors were sufficient for this factor analysis. The p-value is 0.0244, which indicates that we can reject the null hypothesis. Substantively, this means that this factor analysis has identified six patterns of responses: online connections, cultural learning, physical cultural community, language building, online safety, and cultural exposure. These factors are independent of each other.


Reflection and Design Implications

The goal of this study is to look at methods of improving social connections in the Asian community by tying in cultural values. In other words, this study aimed to look into how Asians can connect culturally over a social app and ways to promote cultural awareness in these Asian communities. With the results from the factor analysis, there are six patterns found in this study. Utilizing these patterns found, some possible design methods to promote cultural connectedness in Asian communities would be making it possible for users to cultivate treasurable online connections, improve mutual cultural learning, utilizing physical cultural communities available, engage in language building activities and exercises, emphasizing on online safety, and lastly, accentuating on cultural exposure.

For my design direction, I will focus more on these factors– cultural learning, online connections and safety. Some possible design ideas for "cultural learning" would be the possibility for users to meet people with similar backgrounds, engaging in mutual cultural learning, and to form intimate communities; for the factor "online connections", organizing a way for users to easily make friends individually and be in groups may help the cultivation of online connections; and lastly, for "online safety", giving users an option to report and block potential trolls on the internet may help the users with promoting the feeling of security and control (users can block and report whoever they find troubling).

Reflection

Based on some of the responses from the reflection questions, a lot of the participants had comments about how most online groups aren't geared towards actual learning and just merely meme-tagging. Additionally, many also were afraid that being in an online group or becoming friends with people who have a similar cultural background would lower the diversity of their friend group. Reflection: Wireframing and Usability Testing

I made wireframes using for each scenario per persona. I made sure to follow the scenario as closely as possible, while utilizing the data from the user research and keeping in mind the factors I chose to focus on.

For the usability test, my task for the participant was similar to the scenarios. My first set of tasks were mirrored from June's scenarios, which included: signing up, publish a memoir, meet someone new by sending a message, and lastly, to accept a group invitiation. My second set of tasks were mirrored from Jan's scenarios: logging in, looking for a group, joining it, and inviting a friend to a group.

Overall, the participant for the usability test thought that the wireframes were pretty self-explanatory, such that they made sense, was clear cut and detailed enough to know which part was for what according to the tasks. Some critque and suggestions I recieved was that I should utilize color to organize the categories. In other words, I should use colors to make certain things "stick" out to separate important information and details. Also, from analyzing the participant's feedback and reactions, I decided to change the preference and interest page and split it into two parts. Also, I decided to change my log in/sign up screen to separate the screens instead of combining the two processes at once.

Usability Evaluation

About the Usability Test

The usability test took place in Oakland, CA. The room contained a desk, a laptop, the moderator and the participant. The test was conducted to test how easy and intuitive the designs of the app were in regard to the tasks. The participant was asked to complete six tasks: Signing up and publishing a memoir, meeting someone new and sending them a message, accepting a group invitation, inviting a friend to a group, logging in to search and join a group, and lastly, accepting a match request from a new user.


Major Findings

  • The icons on the navigation bar is too confusing
  • The scrolling screens were a bit weird
  • Participants didn't really know where the functions were at (unintuitive)
  • Some connector issues on Adobe Xd end (can be easily fixed)
  • Arrow on the screen after finishing selecting preferences that points to profile is a bit confusing

Revisions

Tools Used

  • Adobe Xd to revise the interactive prototype

Navigation Bar

Based on the feedback from the usability evaluation, I changed the navigation bar. It was hard to find a good icon with the limited icons, so I thought the message icon and a plus-sign icon would suffice the duty to: meet and message new people, and check for match/message requests


Beginning Screen

I removed the arrow because my participants seemed to hesitate and get confused on whether they should click on the arrow or on the icon from the navigation bar. So, I decided to just remove the arrow.


Scrolling Screens

For the scrolling screen, initially I wasn't too skilled with working with Adobe Xd but later I figured out how to make the navigation bar fixed on the screen for scrolling purposes. So, for the scrolling screens, I moved the navigation bar back to the end of the original sized screen (along with the new navigation bar).


Others

The other issues like the connectors were easily fixed. However, for the icons that made the user confused due to its unintuitive nature, I tried to fix it by changing the icon to the messaging icon, as stated above. Additionally, one of the participants also stated that most new apps take time to learn its functionality, so perhaps with more exposure, the functionalities and layout will become more intuitive.
